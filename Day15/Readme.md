Salesforce Summer Program – Day 15

1. Data Quality Problems

Enterprise systems depend heavily on accurate and reliable data.

Bad Data Examples

1. Duplicate Student Records

Same student exists multiple times.

Business Problem

Incorrect attendance, duplicate notifications, and reporting confusion.

---

2. Missing Email Address

Student email is empty.

Business Problem

Important notifications cannot be delivered.

---

3. Wrong Department Assignment

Student assigned to incorrect department.

Business Problem

Incorrect course allocation and reporting errors.

---

4. Invalid Attendance Percentage

Attendance greater than 100%.

Business Problem

Wrong eligibility calculations and reports.

---

5. Duplicate Course Allocation

Student registered twice for same course.

Business Problem

Seat count becomes inaccurate.

---

6. Incorrect Fee Status

Fee marked as paid when payment failed.

Business Problem

Financial inconsistencies.

---

7. Missing Phone Number

Student contact number not available.

Business Problem

Communication issues during emergencies.

---

8. Incorrect Faculty Assignment

Wrong faculty linked to course.

Business Problem

Class management confusion.

---

9. Invalid Date Format

Different date formats entered incorrectly.

Business Problem

System errors and incorrect scheduling.

---

10. Incomplete Student Records

Important fields left empty.

Business Problem

Unreliable reports and incomplete analytics.

---

2. Migration Discussion

Suppose the college moves from Excel sheets to Salesforce.

Migration Challenges

Duplicate Data

Same student records may exist multiple times.

Missing Data

Old Excel files may have incomplete information.

Inconsistent Formats

Different formats for phone numbers, dates, and names.

Invalid Records

Incorrect values may be imported into Salesforce.

Mapping Errors

Wrong Excel columns may map to incorrect Salesforce fields.

Large Data Volume

Thousands of records may slow migration process.

Proper validation and cleaning are important before migration.

---

3. Duplicate Prevention Ideas

Enterprise systems use different methods to prevent duplicate data.

Validation Rules

Prevent invalid data entry.

Required Fields

Ensure important fields are not empty.

Duplicate Rules

Detect duplicate students or records.

Unique Fields

Use unique email or student ID.

Data Validation

Check formats before saving records.

These methods improve data quality and consistency.

---

4. Enterprise Risks of Bad Data

Suppose 50,000 student records are imported incorrectly.

Wrong Notifications

Students may receive incorrect emails or alerts.

Incorrect Attendance

Attendance reports may become inaccurate.

Fee Issues

Wrong payment status may affect finances.

Reporting Errors

Management reports may show false information.

Course Allocation Problems

Students may be assigned to wrong courses.

Security Risks

Sensitive information may be exposed incorrectly.

Bad data can seriously affect enterprise operations and decision-making.

---

5. Reflection

Clean and reliable data is critical for enterprise systems because all business operations depend on data accuracy.

Good data helps:

- Generate accurate reports
- Improve decision-making
- Maintain reliable automation
- Prevent business errors
- Improve customer and user experience

Poor-quality data can create:

- Financial loss
- Incorrect analytics
- System failures
- Communication problems

Enterprise systems are only as reliable as the quality of their data.
